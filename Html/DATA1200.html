Levanger

Levanger is a beautiful city with its roots in the past. Celebrating its 1000th year in 2011, Levanger is an important port city between the Norwegian Sweden, which has been economically and politically active since the 19th century.It has a rich and diverse nature stretching from the fjord to the high mountain. In the plains, there are cultural landscapes that are very important for many species, and in the eastern parts there are large marsh areas and mountains. Levanger has an unspoiled nature with its rich animal and plant life. It also has beautiful landscapes such as a small boat marina and a city park. Since the past, the fjord has been the main transportation route for passenger traffic and agricultural products are transported. Also, a rare and unspoilt swamp like The Øvre Forra is found in just a few places in Norway.

Levanger River passes through the Levanger elegantly. There is a nice walking and cycling path with seating areas all along the river

Bicycle

Levanger has a large network for cycling around the city. From Torget to Røstad, via Holmgården, you can stop by crossing the harbor on the Snakkarberget bridge and you can visit the Levanger amusement park and Magneten shopping center

Brusve Manor

It was a mansion dating back to 1803 and has been restored. The museum also has the residence of the Reinslyst Laborer built in 1850 inside it. It includes old crafts, a modern art gallery and a local photographer's photo collection.
